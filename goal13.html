Take Urgent Action to Combat Climate Change and Its Impacts

This goal calls for cooperation in building resilience and adaptive capacity to its adverse effects, developing sustainable low-carbon pathways to the future, and accelerating the reduction of global greenhouse gas emissions

  • Targets

targets

Target 13.1

Strengthen resilience and adaptive capacity to climate-related hazards and natural disasters in all countries

Target 13.2

Integrate climate change measures into national policies, strategies and planning

Target 13.3

Improve education, awareness-raising and human and institutional capacity on climate change mitigation, adaptation, impact reduction and early warning

Target 13.a

Implement the commitment undertaken by developed-country parties to the United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change to a goal of mobilizing jointly $100 billion annually by 2020 from all sources to address the needs of developing countries in the context of meaningful mitigation actions and transparency on implementation and fully operationalize the Green Climate Fund through its capitalization as soon as possible

Target 13.b

Promote mechanisms for raising capacity for effective climate change-related planning and management in least developed countries and small island developing States, including focusing on women, youth and local and marginalized communities

survival and recovery assistance program (SURE)

The Survival and Recovery Assistance Program aims to support the government's goal of helping agricultural households in calamity-affected areas regain their capacity to earn a living, and provide immediate relief to small farmers fisherfolk through loan and grant assistance

Type of Loan Assistance and Purpose Recovery Assistance:

To finance the requirements of rehabilitating their farming and/or fishing or livelihood activities includes

  • Production inputs
  • Repair of farm/fishery assets
  • Acquisition of livestock/work animals

    • making cities resilient 2030 (mcr2030)

    MCR2030 is a initiative for improving local resilience through advocacy, sharing knowledge and experiences, establishing mutually reinforcing city-to-city learning networks, injecting technical expertise, connecting multiple layers of government, and building partnerships

    Strategic Objectives
  • Increase city understanding of risk and commitments to disaster risk reduction and resilience
  • Increase city capacities to plan for risk reduction and resilience
  • Increase city capacities to implement resilience actions and reduce risks
